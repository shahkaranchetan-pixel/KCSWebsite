Zoho Books Pricing in India (2026): Plans & Costs Explained

A plain-English guide to every Zoho Books plan, what each one includes, the GST features that matter, and the real total cost of running your books - from a certified Zoho partner CA firm in Mumbai.

Zoho Books Pricing at a Glance

Zoho Books offers a tiered pricing structure in India, from a Free plan for the smallest businesses up to enterprise-grade plans with deep automation and analytics. Each step up adds users, automation and advanced features. The table below summarises the plans and who they suit.

Is There a Free Plan? (And Who Qualifies)

Yes - Zoho Books has a genuinely useful Free plan in India, aimed at the smallest businesses. It is available to businesses with annual turnover up to ₹25 lakh and includes core invoicing, expense and accounting features for a single user plus your accountant. It is an excellent starting point for solo founders and very early-stage businesses. As you cross the ₹25 lakh turnover limit or need more users and automation, you move to a paid plan.

Monthly vs Annual Billing

Zoho Books, like most SaaS, is cheaper when billed annually versus monthly - the annual commitment typically carries a meaningful discount. If you are confident Zoho Books is the right long-term fit (which is easy to validate with the Free plan or a trial first), annual billing lowers your effective monthly cost. If you are still testing, start monthly and switch once you have decided.

What's Actually Included for GST

Because Zoho Books India is built specifically for Indian compliance, GST is core to the product rather than a paid add-on. Across the plans you typically get:

  • GST-compliant invoicing with correct HSN/SAC codes and place-of-supply logic.
  • GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B return preparation and filing support directly from your books.
  • e-Invoicing (IRN) and e-way bills for businesses that cross the applicable thresholds.
  • Input Tax Credit reconciliation against GSTR-2B to keep your ITC claims clean.

Exactly which GST features are available depends on the plan, so check the inclusions for the tier you are considering. Read our step-by-step guide to filing GST returns on Zoho Books.

The Real Total Cost of Ownership

The subscription price is only part of the picture. When budgeting for Zoho Books, factor in the full total cost of ownership:

Setup & Migration

Chart of accounts design, data migration from Tally/Excel, GST configuration, opening balances and user training - a one-time cost that determines how well the system runs afterwards.

Extra Users & Add-Ons

Additional users beyond your plan's limit, and add-ons like advanced automation or higher transaction volumes, carry incremental cost.

Ongoing Bookkeeping

Software records what you enter - someone still has to maintain the books, reconcile and file. This is the largest recurring cost, whether in-house or outsourced.

Compliance Risk

The hidden cost of getting it wrong - GST/TDS errors, late filings and penalties - which the right setup and oversight eliminate.

For many small businesses, the most economical option is the right Zoho Books plan paired with outsourced accounting - which usually costs less than the software plus a full-time in-house accountant, while removing compliance risk entirely. See how accounting outsourcing costs compare.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there a free version of Zoho Books in India?

Yes. Zoho Books offers a Free plan in India aimed at small businesses below a turnover threshold (historically businesses with annual turnover under ₹25 lakh). It includes core invoicing and accounting for a single user plus an accountant. Always confirm the current eligibility limit and feature set on Zoho's official India pricing page before deciding.

Which Zoho Books plan is right for my business?

It depends on transaction volume, number of users, and whether you need workflow automation, e-invoicing, budgeting or advanced analytics. Most small businesses start on Standard or Professional; growing SMEs needing multi-user access and automation move to Premium and above. We help you map your requirements to the right plan so you don't overpay or outgrow it too fast.

Does Zoho Books pricing include GST and e-invoicing?

Zoho Books India is built for GST and includes GST-compliant invoicing, GSTR filing support and e-invoicing/e-way bill features, with availability varying by plan. Confirm which GST features are bundled in the specific plan you are considering on the official pricing page.

What is the real total cost of running Zoho Books?

The subscription is only one part. The real total cost of ownership also includes initial setup and data migration, any extra user or add-on costs, and the ongoing accounting work to actually maintain the books. For many businesses, pairing the right plan with outsourced accounting is cheaper than the software plus an in-house accountant.
