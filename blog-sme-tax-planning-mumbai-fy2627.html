Tax Planning for SMEs in Mumbai: A Complete Guide for FY 2026-27

Published on May 29, 2026
8 min read

For Small and Medium Enterprises (SMEs) operating in the high-cost environment of Mumbai, effective tax planning is not merely about staying compliant; it's a vital cash flow management strategy. As we navigate the financial year 2026-27, tax laws continue to evolve, offering both new challenges and lucrative opportunities for business owners to optimize their tax liabilities.

1. Embrace the Presumptive Taxation Scheme

If your SME has a turnover of less than ₹3 Crores (under Section 44AD) or gross receipts of less than ₹75 Lakhs for professionals (under Section 44ADA) — provided your cash receipts don't exceed 5% — opting for presumptive taxation can drastically reduce your compliance burden. You declare profits at a fixed percentage and are exempt from maintaining exhaustive books of accounts or undergoing a tax audit.

2. Maximize Depreciation Benefits

Investing in new machinery, computers, or software? Ensure you are claiming the correct depreciation rates. The Income Tax Act allows for accelerated depreciation in specific cases, particularly for energy-saving devices or newly installed plant and machinery. A proactive CA in Mumbai can help you time your capital expenditures perfectly before March 31st to claim half or full depreciation for the year.

"Tax planning should be a year-round conversation with your CA, not a panic-driven exercise in the final week of March."

3. Strategize Directors' Remuneration

If your SME operates as a Private Limited Company, taking money out of the company can be heavily taxed via dividend distribution. Structuring compensation properly—by paying reasonable directors' remuneration (which is a deductible expense for the company) and utilizing the new tax regime benefits at the individual level—is one of the most effective ways to lower the overall tax burden for founders.

4. Leverage Section 80JJAA for Job Creation

Many SMEs are completely unaware of Section 80JJAA. If your business is subject to tax audit and you are expanding your team, you can claim a massive 30% additional deduction on the salaries paid to new regular employees for three consecutive years. For growing startups in Mumbai, this is a golden ticket to lower corporate taxes while expanding operations.

5. Accurate TDS Deductions and Reconciliations

The Income Tax Department is increasingly leveraging AI to cross-verify TDS returns with the Annual Information Statement (AIS). Failing to deduct TDS, or deducting it under the wrong section, can result in 30% of your business expenses being disallowed. Partnering with an outsourced accounting firm ensures your TDS payments and return filings are flawless.
