Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need any prior knowledge to take this course?

No prior accounting or finance knowledge is required. This course starts from absolute basics and builds up systematically. A basic understanding of arithmetic is all you need.

What is the difference between cash basis and accrual basis accounting?

Cash basis records income when cash is received and expenses when cash is paid. Accrual basis records income when earned and expenses when incurred, regardless of cash movement. Accrual basis is mandatory for most businesses in India.

After this course, which software should I learn?

Start with Zoho Books Basics or Tally Prime Basics — both are practical applications of the accounting concepts taught in this course. Knowing the theory first makes the software much easier to understand.

What is the difference between a ledger and a journal?

A Journal is the book of original entry where transactions are first recorded in chronological order. A Ledger is the book of secondary entry where transactions are classified by account (e.g., all Sales entries in the Sales ledger).

Are the three golden rules of accounting still relevant in modern software?

Yes. Even though accounting software handles debits and credits automatically, understanding the golden rules helps you catch errors, understand reports and set up your chart of accounts correctly.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Rule 11UA and when does it apply?

Rule 11UA prescribes the valuation method for unquoted equity shares under the Income Tax Act. It applies when shares are issued to investors above fair market value (Angel Tax provision) or transferred. A registered valuer's report is required.

What credentials does a business valuer need in India?

For IBBI-compliant valuation reports (mandatory for insolvency, M&A and regulated transactions), the valuer must be a Registered Valuer (RV) under IBBI. Our founder CA Karan Shah holds IBBI registration as a Registered Valuer.

When is a business valuation report required?

Valuations are required for: fundraising (determining share issuance price), M&A (transaction pricing), ESOP grants (Rule 11UA), FEMA compliance (FDI/ODI), NCLT petitions, corporate restructuring and IBC insolvency proceedings.

What is the difference between enterprise value and equity value?

Enterprise Value (EV) is the total value of the business including debt and excluding cash. Equity Value = Enterprise Value - Net Debt. When valuing for an equity investor, you always need the equity value, not just EV.

How accurate are DCF valuations?

DCF is theoretically sound but highly sensitive to assumptions — small changes in WACC or growth rate significantly change the output. That is why valuers triangulate using multiple methods (DCF + CCA + Asset-based) and exercise professional judgment.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between CMA data and a business plan?

A business plan is a narrative document explaining the business model, market and strategy. CMA data is a structured financial document in a specific RBI-prescribed format that banks use to assess creditworthiness and set loan limits.

For how many years are CMA projections typically prepared?

Banks typically require projections for 3-5 years for term loans and 1-2 years of projections for working capital facilities, along with 2-3 years of audited historical financials.

What is the Tandon Committee Method for MPBF calculation?

The Tandon Committee prescribed three methods for calculating working capital finance. Method II (most commonly used) permits bank finance up to 75% of the net working capital gap (Current Assets minus current liabilities other than bank finance).

Can startups with no revenue apply for bank loans with CMA data?

Most banks require some operating history. However, government-backed schemes like CGTMSE or SIDBI loans, and MUDRA loans, may be available for new businesses. Projection-based CMA data is accepted for these schemes.

Who prepares CMA data — the business owner or a CA?

CMA data is typically prepared by a CA or a financial consultant with banking and credit knowledge. Banks often reject self-prepared CMA data that does not meet their internal standards. Our Corporate Finance team prepares CMA data regularly.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to incorporate a Pvt Ltd company in India?

With all documents in order, a Pvt Ltd company can be incorporated in 10-15 working days through the MCA portal. Our team at KC Shah & Associates can complete the process end-to-end.

What is the minimum share capital required for a Pvt Ltd company?

There is no minimum paid-up capital requirement for a Pvt Ltd company in India. You can start with ₹1. The authorised capital determines your registration and stamp duty fees.

What is the difference between a Director and a Shareholder?

A Director manages the company's day-to-day affairs and is appointed by shareholders. A Shareholder (Member) owns equity in the company. In small companies, the same person is often both.

Can a foreigner be a director or shareholder in an Indian Pvt Ltd company?

Yes. A foreign national can be a shareholder and director in an Indian Pvt Ltd company. For FDI (Foreign Direct Investment), additional FEMA compliance and FC-GPR filing is required — our FEMA service covers this.

What compliances are required after incorporating a company?

After incorporation: open a current bank account, apply for PAN and TAN, register for GST (if applicable), appoint an auditor within 30 days, file AOC-4 and MGT-7 annually and hold board meetings. Our ROC compliance service covers all of these.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need VBA to build Excel dashboards?

No. This course teaches you to build fully interactive dashboards using Pivot Tables, slicers, named ranges and chart tricks — no VBA required.

What data sources can feed an Excel dashboard?

Excel dashboards can pull data from Zoho Books exports, Tally reports, bank statements, CRM exports or any CSV file. Power Query (covered in our Advanced Excel course) is the best way to connect and refresh these sources.

Is Excel better than Power BI for dashboards?

For simple, shareable dashboards that anyone can open, Excel is often better — no software installation needed, works offline and is universally understood. Power BI is more powerful for large-scale, real-time data.

Can I share an Excel dashboard with someone who does not have Excel?

Yes. You can export the dashboard as a PDF for read-only sharing, or share via Microsoft 365/OneDrive so others can view it in the browser without installing Excel.

What colour scheme is recommended for a professional business dashboard?

Use a maximum of 3 colours: one primary (your brand colour), one accent for highlights, and neutral greys for backgrounds and borders. Avoid rainbow charts — they reduce clarity.

How do I value my startup for the first fundraise?

What is the difference between equity funding and debt funding?

Equity funding means giving investors a share of ownership in exchange for capital — no repayment obligation. Debt funding is a loan that must be repaid with interest, regardless of business performance.

What documents should a startup have ready before approaching investors?

A pitch deck, financial model/projections, cap table, company incorporation documents, IP assignments, key contracts, audited financials (if available) and a data room with all company documents.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the current GST rate for most services in India?

Most services attract 18% GST. Some services like healthcare and education are exempt, restaurants charge 5%, and financial services have specific rates. Always check the GST rate schedule for specific items.

Can a small business with turnover below ₹20 lakh voluntarily register for GST?

Yes. Voluntary GST registration is allowed even below the threshold. It can be beneficial if you have B2B clients who need to claim ITC, or if you export services.

What is the E-Invoice requirement?

Businesses with annual turnover above ₹5 crore must generate e-invoices through the IRP (Invoice Registration Portal) for B2B and export invoices. The system generates an IRN and QR code that must appear on the invoice.

What is the difference between GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B?

GSTR-1 contains detailed invoice-wise data of all outward supplies. GSTR-3B is a summary return showing consolidated tax liability and ITC. You must file GSTR-1 before GSTR-3B each month.

What is the composition scheme and who should opt for it?

The composition scheme lets small businesses (turnover up to ₹1.5 crore for goods, ₹50 lakh for services) pay a flat GST rate (1-5%) instead of regular GST. Simpler compliance but you cannot collect GST from customers or claim ITC.

What is the government fee for incorporating a Pvt Ltd company?

Government fees depend on authorised share capital — approximately ₹2,000—₹15,000 for stamp duty and filing fees for typical startup share capital levels. Professional fees are additional.

What is the difference between authorised capital and paid-up capital?

Authorised capital is the maximum share capital the company can issue (determines filing fees). Paid-up capital is the actual capital invested by shareholders. You can start with ₹1 paid-up capital.

Yes, you need a registered office address in India. This can be your home address initially. Proof of address (electricity bill) and a No Objection Certificate from the property owner are required.

That is perfectly fine. Multiple directors can share the same address for their personal address (as used in the DIN application). The registered office address is the company's address, which is separate.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it mandatory to file an ITR even if my income is below the taxable limit?

Filing is not mandatory if income is below the basic exemption limit. However, it is advisable to file a Nil return — it serves as proof of income, helps get loans and visas, and enables carry forward of losses.

What is the difference between gross total income and total income?

Gross Total Income is the sum of all income heads before Chapter VI-A deductions. Total Income is Gross Total Income minus allowable deductions (80C, 80D, etc.) — this is what tax is calculated on.

What happens if I miss the ITR filing deadline?

You can file a belated return up to 31st December of the assessment year with a late filing fee (₹1,000 if income is below ₹5 lakh, ₹5,000 otherwise). Interest under Section 234A also applies.

Can I switch between Old and New Tax Regime every year?

Salaried individuals and pensioners can switch between regimes every year. Self-employed individuals and those with business income can switch only once and cannot revert (except if they opt out of the new regime permanently).

What is the Section 87A rebate?

Under the New Regime, if taxable income is = ₹12 lakh, the full income tax is rebated (up to ₹60,000). Under the Old Regime, if taxable income is = ₹5 lakh, tax up to ₹12,500 is rebated.

Do companies still use Excel for MIS or have they moved to software?

What is the difference between a P&L and an MIS report?

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Tally Prime calculate PF and ESI automatically?

Yes, once you configure the PF and ESI pay heads with the correct rates and link them to employees, Tally automatically calculates deductions when you process the payroll voucher.

Can I generate Form 16 (Part B) from Tally Prime?

Tally Prime generates the salary TDS data but Form 16 (Part A with TDS certificate) must be generated from the TRACES portal using TDS return data. Module 5 explains the complete process.

How does Tally handle employees joining mid-month?

You can set the joining date in the employee master and Tally will pro-rate the salary for the number of days worked in that month automatically.

Can Tally Prime handle more than 100 employees?

Yes, Tally Prime handles payroll for any number of employees. There is no employee limit in the software — performance depends on your hardware.

After this course, should I switch to Zoho Payroll instead of Tally?

For cloud-based companies using Zoho Books, Zoho Payroll is better integrated. For businesses already on Tally with on-premise data, Tally payroll is the natural fit. Our Zoho Payroll Basics course covers the alternative.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do individuals need to deduct TDS?

Individuals are generally not required to deduct TDS unless they are subject to tax audit under Section 44AB, or make payments that attract TDS under Section 194IB (rent above ₹50,000/month), 194M or 194S.

What is the difference between TDS and advance tax?

TDS is deducted by the payer on behalf of the recipient and deposited to the government. Advance tax is paid by the taxpayer directly in four installments during the year based on estimated income.

What happens if TDS is deducted but not deposited on time?

Interest of 1.5% per month is levied for late deposit. Additionally, a penalty under Section 271C can be imposed equal to the TDS amount not deposited.

What is Form 16 vs Form 16A?

Form 16 is issued by employers to salaried employees for TDS on salary (Section 192). Form 16A is issued by companies and individuals for TDS deducted on non-salary payments like interest, professional fees and contract payments.

How do I claim credit for TDS deducted on my income?

When filing your ITR, enter the TDS details from your Form 16/16A and 26AS. The TDS credit is automatically adjusted against your income tax liability and any excess is refunded.

What is the TDS rate under Section 194C for contractors?

What is the threshold limit for TDS under Section 194J?

What if a vendor provides a lower TDS deduction certificate?

If a vendor provides a certificate under Section 197 from the IT department, you must deduct TDS at the lower rate specified in the certificate instead of the standard rate.

Interest at 1% per month for non-deduction and 1.5% per month for non-deposit. Additionally, the expense may be disallowed in your income tax filing under Section 40a(ia).

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I learn VLOOKUP or XLOOKUP first?

Start with VLOOKUP since most existing spreadsheets use it and you will encounter it frequently. Then learn XLOOKUP as it is more flexible and is the modern replacement. This course covers both.

Is XLOOKUP available in all versions of Excel?

XLOOKUP is available in Excel 365 and Excel 2021 and later. It is not available in Excel 2016 or 2019. For older versions, use INDEX-MATCH as the equivalent.

How is VLOOKUP used in GST filing work?

VLOOKUP is commonly used to look up GST rates from an HSN code table, match invoices with GSTR-2A data, or pull vendor details from a master list — all covered in Module 6.

What is the advantage of INDEX-MATCH over VLOOKUP?

INDEX-MATCH can look up values in any column (not just the first), handles left-side lookups, works with dynamic column references and is generally faster on large datasets.

Can XLOOKUP return multiple columns at once?

Yes. XLOOKUP can return multiple adjacent columns simultaneously by specifying a multi-column return range — this is one of its biggest advantages over VLOOKUP.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is Zoho Inventory different from Zoho Books?

Zoho Books is an accounting software focused on financial transactions. Zoho Inventory focuses on stock management — tracking item quantities, warehouse locations and order fulfilment. They integrate tightly.

Can Zoho Inventory sync with Amazon or Flipkart?

Yes. Zoho Inventory integrates with Amazon, Flipkart, Shopify and other e-commerce platforms to centralise order and inventory management.

Is Zoho Inventory suitable for a small product-based business?

Yes. Zoho Inventory is well-suited for small to mid-sized product businesses. The free plan supports up to 50 orders per month.

How does Zoho Inventory handle GST on purchase orders?

Zoho Inventory automatically applies GST based on the item's HSN code and the vendor's GST registration. When integrated with Zoho Books, ITC is automatically tracked.

What report should I check to know which items are running low?

The "Inventory Summary" report shows current stock levels for all items. You can also set reorder points in Zoho Inventory to get alerts when stock falls below a threshold.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Zoho Payroll handle PF and ESI filing automatically?

Zoho Payroll calculates PF and ESI deductions automatically. For actual filing, you need to download the ECR file and upload it to the EPFO portal, or use Zoho Payroll's direct filing feature available on higher plans.

Can Zoho Payroll integrate with Zoho Books?

Yes. Once payroll is processed in Zoho Payroll, journal entries are automatically posted to Zoho Books, saving time and eliminating manual reconciliation.

Is Zoho Payroll compliant with Indian labour laws?

Zoho Payroll is built for Indian compliance — covering PF, ESI, PT, TDS (Section 192), LWF and gratuity calculations. It is updated regularly when statutory rates change.

How many employees can I manage on Zoho Payroll?

Zoho Payroll supports any number of employees. Pricing is per employee per month, starting from ₹40/employee/month.

What is the difference between CTC, Gross Salary and Net Salary?

CTC (Cost to Company) includes all employer costs including PF contribution. Gross Salary is the total before deductions. Net Salary (take-home) is after deducting PF, ESI, TDS and PT.
